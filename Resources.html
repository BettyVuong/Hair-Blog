Overview

As a self-taught hairstylist, I needed to keep up with newer hair trends and improve my technique but, you can't find a lot of these things in a textbook or easily online. When I first started out, I had to dig so far on youtube and the internet to find something decent. Learning a lot of these techniques require going to classes hosted by popular hairstylists. They're very talented but so many are based in the USA so how could I travel as a teen and pay for those classes? Overtime, I also found hairstylists who post educational content. Following them online allowed me to passively learn the little tricks from their years mastering their craft as I would scroll through instagram. There is also a lot to observe from their videos as well. The one thing I find that is difficult is colour formulations, every hairstylist uses a different colour line or caters to a specific clientele. Here is a guide of resources that you should use beyond my blog to learn more and master your craft!

Conclusion

A lot of these resources are hair colouring centered but that's my specialty so it makes sense! To look at more foundational and general resources I heavily recommend Ms Makeover because she covers a lot of fundamentals in cosmetology courses since she is a cosmetology teacher. Another resource is Kenra Professional for tutorials, they cover cuts, colour, product, and styling. To have an even clearer perspective on theory going past cosmetology schools, Julie Kleine - colorswithchemistry is a fantastic educator that brings complexity and clarity to her work.

Have fun learning tutorials and enriching yourself!
